summary: >-
  Apple sued OpenAI in federal court on July 10, alleging trade secret
  misappropriation and breach of contract tied to OpenAI's unreleased AI
  hardware device. The suit names two former Apple engineers — one who
  allegedly downloaded confidential hardware files and kept a
  work-issued laptop after joining OpenAI — plus io Products, the
  startup founded by ex-Apple design chief Jony Ive that OpenAI acquired
  last year; Ive himself is not accused of wrongdoing. OpenAI said it
  has "no interest in other companies' trade secrets."
implications: >-
  The suit turns on specific, documented conduct — an unreturned laptop,
  downloaded files, skipped exit procedures — not just competitive
  overlap, which raises the stakes for any company building close
  product partnerships or hiring a rival's engineers. It also threatens
  to delay both OpenAI's previously teased AI hardware device and its
  widely anticipated IPO.
