summary: >-
  On July 17, Apple's market value climbed to about $4.88 trillion, edging
  past Nvidia's roughly $4.84 trillion after Nvidia shares dropped nearly
  3.5% intraday — briefly handing Apple the title it last held before
  Nvidia overtook Microsoft in June 2025. The lead didn't hold: Nvidia
  closed the day slightly ahead. Apple is up roughly 23% in 2026 on
  investor enthusiasm for its AI product plans and lighter AI capital
  spending, while Nvidia has gained only about 9% as Wall Street rotates
  toward the memory-chip and infrastructure side of the AI buildout.
implications: >-
  Even a brief swap signals a real shift in how investors are pricing the
  AI trade — rewarding companies with lower direct AI capital exposure and
  strong existing products over the chipmakers that powered the initial
  buildout. For businesses tracking AI infrastructure costs, it's a sign
  the compute-layer economics may be entering a more volatile, less
  one-directional phase than the last two years.
