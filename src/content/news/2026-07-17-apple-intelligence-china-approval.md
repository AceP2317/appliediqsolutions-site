  rather than shipping its own AI into China — a concession forced by more
  than two years of regulatory delay in one of its largest markets. It's a
  concrete case of a US platform company running two different AI stacks in
  two different jurisdictions, with the model choice itself dictated by
  where the regulator sits rather than by what the company would otherwise
  ship.
