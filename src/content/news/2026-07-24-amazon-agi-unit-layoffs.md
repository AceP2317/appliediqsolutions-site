summary: >-
  Amazon confirmed Wednesday it laid off an undisclosed number of employees
  in its artificial general intelligence unit, which builds the Nova model
  family and also houses silicon and quantum-computing groups. Employees
  focused on model customization and post-training were among those cut,
  according to LinkedIn posts first reported by Reuters. An Amazon
  spokesperson said the company is "sharpening our focus on the initiatives
  that matter most for customers," even as it keeps investing heavily in AI
  infrastructure; the cuts follow more than 30,000 layoffs company-wide
  since October.
implications: >-
  A company spending tens of billions on AI infrastructure is simultaneously
  cutting the research staff that builds its own foundation models — a sign
  even the largest labs are narrowing which parts of AI development they do
  in-house versus buy, a calculation worth watching for any business
  weighing its own build-vs-buy AI decisions.
