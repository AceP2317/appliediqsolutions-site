title: 'Nvidia cuts its authorized Asian AI-chip buyer list in half'

summary: >-
  Nvidia has more than halved the number of Asian customers authorized to
  buy its AI chips after introducing a stricter "white list" of buyers who
  pass tougher compliance checks meant to stop the chips from reaching
  China through third countries, the Financial Times reported (via the
  Manila Times). Over recent months Nvidia stepped up due diligence in
  Singapore, Malaysia, and Japan — visiting customer data centers,
  verifying contracts, and interviewing end users — and most previous
  customers, particularly neo-cloud providers, failed the review and were
  dropped, though they can reapply. The move follows a May Commerce
  Department directive targeting routes for US chips to reach China.
implications: >-
  Access to frontier AI chips now runs through a compliance gate that can
  remove a buyer with little warning, on top of the existing wait for
  supply. Any business whose AI plans depend on a specific Asian cloud or
  neo-cloud provider should confirm that provider is still on Nvidia's list
  rather than assume continuity — this kind of verification is exactly the
  discipline physical supply chains have long applied to their own vendors,
  now extended to AI compute.
