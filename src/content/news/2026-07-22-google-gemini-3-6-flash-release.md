summary: >-
  Google released Gemini 3.6 Flash, Gemini 3.5 Flash-Lite, and a
  security-focused Gemini 3.5 Flash Cyber model, its first major model
  updates since May's I/O event. Gemini 3.6 Flash uses 17% fewer output
  tokens than its predecessor while costing less ($1.50/1M input and
  $7.50/1M output tokens, versus $9/1M output before) and posts real coding
  gains, including 49% versus 37% on the DeepSWE benchmark, plus a knowledge
  cutoff that advances to March 2026. Gemini 3.5 Pro remains in partner
  testing with no release date, and Google says it has begun pre-training
  Gemini 4.
implications: >-
  For teams already on Gemini, this is a straightforward cost-and-quality
  upgrade at the efficient end of the lineup rather than a frontier leap —
  the token efficiency and pricing cuts matter most for high-volume agentic
  and coding workloads where per-call cost adds up fast. The continued gap
  on 3.5 Pro is the more telling detail: Google is still visibly behind
  Anthropic and OpenAI on the high-end coding tier it has promised since
  May.
