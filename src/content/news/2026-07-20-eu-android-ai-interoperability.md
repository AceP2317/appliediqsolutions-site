title: 'EU orders Google to open Android to rival AI assistants, share Search data'

summary: >-
  The European Commission issued two binding Digital Markets Act
  specification measures against Google on July 16. The first requires
  Google to give rival AI assistants the same Android access Gemini has —
  voice-command activation, delegating in-app actions like booking a taxi,
  and reply suggestions in chat apps. The second requires Google to share
  anonymized Search data with competing search engines and AI chatbots,
  using an anonymization method built with internal and external privacy
  experts. The Commission says restricted access currently leaves rival
  assistants unable to compete on equal footing on the roughly 60% of EU
  users who carry an Android device.
implications: >-
  It's a live precedent that a platform gatekeeper can be forced to open
  access at the operating-system level, not just the app-store level —
  giving third-party AI assistants a real on-ramp to Android's installed
  base instead of restricted access. The changes phase in on a real
  timeline, not immediately: search-data sharing phases in through 2026
  with pricing finalized by January 2027, while the Android access changes
  aren't required until Android 18 ships by August 2027.
