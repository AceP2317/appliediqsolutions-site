summary: >-
  Xi Jinping announced the formal creation of the World Artificial
  Intelligence Cooperation Organisation (WAICO) at the World AI Conference
  in Shanghai, an intergovernmental body headquartered in China with 29
  founding member states including Indonesia, Brazil, Malaysia, South
  Africa, Senegal, Russia and Pakistan. Xi called for AI development that
  is not "a solo performance by a single country" and framed the
  organization around equitable access to AI for the Global South, in
  what analysts described as an implicit rebuke of US dominance in AI
  policy and standard-setting.
implications: >-
  A second pole of AI governance forming outside US and EU frameworks
  means businesses operating internationally may eventually face two
  divergent regulatory regimes rather than one converging set of norms,
  especially selling into the Global South markets WAICO courts. It's
  early — the organization has no binding authority yet — but it signals
  where China intends to compete next: not just model capability, but who
  writes the rules other countries adopt.
