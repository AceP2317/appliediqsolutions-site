summary: >-
  The European Commission published its final guidelines on Article 50 of
  the AI Act, the transparency rules that take effect August 2, 2026. They
  clarify which providers and deployers must disclose that a user is
  interacting with an AI system, apply machine-readable marking to
  AI-generated or altered content, flag deepfakes and AI-generated
  public-interest text published without human review, and notify people
  exposed to emotion-recognition or biometric-categorization systems.
implications: >-
  Any business selling into the EU or running AI-facing tools there now has
  a hard compliance deadline two weeks out, with fines up to 15 million
  euros or 3% of global turnover for violations — worth checking now whether
  chatbots, AI-generated marketing content, or biometric features need new
  disclosures before August 2.
