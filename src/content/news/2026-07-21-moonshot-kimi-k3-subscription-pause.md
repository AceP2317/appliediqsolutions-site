summary: >-
  Days after Moonshot AI released its 2.8-trillion-parameter Kimi K3 model
  on July 16, the company paused new subscriptions after user request
  volume in the first 48 hours pushed close to the limit of its GPU
  capacity, saying it needed to protect the experience of existing
  subscribers. Existing subscribers are unaffected, and Moonshot plans to
  reopen signups in batches while splitting its membership into separate
  general-use and coding-focused tiers to better match compute to demand.
implications: >-
  A model can top benchmark charts and still hit a hard capacity wall
  within days of launch — a reminder that the compute behind a rented API
  is finite and can throttle access on short notice, independent of how
  good the model itself is. Kimi K3's full weights are still due open-
  source by July 27, which would let users who hit this kind of limit run
  the model on their own infrastructure instead.
angle: >-
  This is the exposure that comes with renting rather than owning: access
  to a vendor-hosted model can be paused by that vendor's own capacity
  crunch, on their timeline, not yours. A tool built and hosted for one
  business doesn't carry that risk.
