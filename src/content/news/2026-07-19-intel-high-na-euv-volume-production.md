summary: >-
  ASML announced that Intel Foundry has entered high-volume production of
  select layers on its Core Ultra Series 3 ("Panther Lake") chips using
  ASML's High NA EUV lithography — a next-generation patterning technology
  offering finer resolution than the EUV tools the industry has used to
  date. The affected 18A-node layers are "dual-qualified," shipping to
  customers at yields matched to Intel's existing EUV line, and the run is
  described as a readiness step feeding into Intel's next node, 14A, which
  is designed to use High NA EUV more extensively.
implications: >-
  Being first to run High NA EUV at volume, even on a subset of layers,
  gives Intel real production data — uptime, overlay accuracy, yield —
  that rivals like TSMC and Samsung don't yet have, at a moment when
  leading-edge lithography capacity is one of the tightest constraints on
  AI chip supply. It's a manufacturing-process milestone, not a new chip
  announcement, but process-node execution is exactly what determines
  whether AI hardware supply can keep pace with demand.
