summary: >-
  Microsoft and AMD announced that Microsoft will deploy AMD's Helios
  rack-scale AI accelerator in volume across its own data centers and Azure,
  to run frontier-model training and inference workloads including managed
  compute through Microsoft Foundry. Each Helios rack links 72 next-
  generation Instinct MI455X GPUs with 31.1TB of combined HBM4 memory,
  delivering up to 2.9 exaFLOPS of FP4 compute; Azure will also add two new
  VM series built on AMD's upcoming Epyc Venice CPUs. Neither company
  disclosed the deployment's size in dollars or watts.
implications: >-
  It's a real diversification move by the industry's largest cloud AI buyer,
  not just AMD marketing — a hedge against depending on a single GPU
  supplier while demand for AI compute keeps outrunning supply. For any
  business whose AI costs run through Azure, more competition at the
  hardware layer is one of the few forces that can push cloud AI pricing
  down over time.
