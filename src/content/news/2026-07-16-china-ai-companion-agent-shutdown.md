summary: >-
  China's rules on "anthropomorphic" AI interaction took effect July 15, and
  ByteDance's Doubao and Alibaba's Qwen moved to disable their
  customized-agent features ahead of it, per the South China Morning Post.
  Both apps had let users turn a general chatbot into a named assistant,
  tutor, or companion with a fixed persona; Doubao keeps old chats
  read-only until October 15, while Qwen gave no grace period before
  deleting agent data outright. The rules target services that "simulate
  human personality traits" for "sustained emotional interaction," citing
  addiction and mental-health risks, and exempt plain customer-service bots.
implications: >-
  This is a narrow carve-out — companion-style personas, not AI agents or
  chatbots generally — but it's a concrete example of a feature hundreds of
  millions of people used disappearing on a government's timeline, with one
  of the two companies giving users no window to export their own data
  first. Any business building customer-facing conversational AI, anywhere,
  is watching an early test case for how regulators plan to draw the line
  around AI systems designed to feel human.
