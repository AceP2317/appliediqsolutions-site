tier: 'standard'
spotlight: false
summary: >-
  OpenAI introduced Presence, a platform for deploying customer-facing AI
  voice and chat agents with policies, guardrails, approved actions,
  simulations, and a Codex-powered improvement loop that reviews production
  interactions and suggests behavior changes for staff to approve. OpenAI
  says Presence already runs its own English-language phone support line,
  resolving 75% of inbound calls without a human, and is rolling out through
  a limited enterprise program via OpenAI's own deployment engineers and
  select partners rather than self-service.
implications: >-
  It's a preview of how the big labs plan to sell agentic AI to businesses:
  not a raw model API but a fully managed deployment service, complete with
  OpenAI's own engineers doing the integration work. For operators evaluating
  AI-agent vendors, that also means the guardrails and escalation logic that
  determine what the agent is allowed to do live inside OpenAI's platform,
  not the customer's own systems.
angle: >-
  It's the vendor-managed alternative to owning your automation outright:
  the guardrails, escalation rules, and improvement loop all live inside
  OpenAI's platform rather than in code the business controls, which is
  exactly the tradeoff durable, in-house-owned tooling is built to avoid.
