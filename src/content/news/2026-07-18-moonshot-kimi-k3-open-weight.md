summary: >-
  Moonshot AI quietly flipped kimi.com overnight to release Kimi K3, a
  2.8-trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts model it's calling the first
  "open 3T-class" model, with full open weights promised by July 27. The
  company's self-reported benchmarks show K3 beating Claude Opus 4.8 max and
  GPT-5.5 high on most measures while trailing Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6
  Sol; independent evaluator Artificial Analysis put its long-horizon
  knowledge-work score 732 points above Moonshot's prior model. It's priced
  at $3/$15 per million tokens — roughly triple its predecessor and the
  most expensive model a Chinese lab has released to date.
implications: >-
  The gap between frontier US labs and openly available Chinese models keeps
  narrowing on both capability and price discipline — Moonshot raised its
  own price sharply here rather than racing to the bottom, betting the
  model can justify it. For businesses, each release like this widens the
  practical menu of models capable enough for real work, including ones
  whose weights can eventually be run privately rather than rented from a
  single vendor's API.
