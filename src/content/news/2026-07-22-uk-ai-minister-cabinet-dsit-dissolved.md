summary: >-
  New UK prime minister Andy Burnham has appointed Kanishka Narayan as AI
  minister with a seat at cabinet — the first time the role has carried
  that rank — while dissolving the Department for Science, Innovation and
  Technology as a standalone body. DSIT's responsibilities move into a new
  Department for Business, Innovation, Science and Trade under Jonathan
  Reynolds, and outgoing DSIT secretary Liz Kendall publicly warned on her
  way out against losing momentum on tech-driven growth.
implications: >-
  Elevating AI to cabinet rank while folding its home department into a
  business ministry is a bet that AI policy belongs next to industrial
  strategy rather than science policy. UK tech and semiconductor industry
  leaders welcomed the cabinet seat but flagged the reshuffle itself as a
  source of near-term delay on national AI infrastructure initiatives —
  worth watching for any business tracking UK AI policy or funding
  programs.
