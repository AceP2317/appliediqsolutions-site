summary: >-
  Z.AI, the Chinese lab behind the GLM model family (formerly Zhipu), has
  completed construction of a 1-gigawatt-class data center powered
  entirely by Chinese-made chips, with part of the facility already
  running. Its power draw is roughly what it takes to supply 750,000
  homes at any given moment, and the site is meant to train and develop
  Z.AI's next GLM systems. The company already runs several other
  computing clusters of more than 10,000 chips each, none of them
  Nvidia's.
implications: >-
  It's a concrete milestone in China's push to build frontier-scale AI
  compute without restricted Nvidia silicon, alongside domestic chipmakers
  like Huawei, Cambricon, and Alibaba working to close the gap. For any
  business whose AI plans touch non-US hardware or vendors, it's a sign
  the compute layer is fragmenting into separate US- and China-anchored
  supply chains rather than converging on one.
