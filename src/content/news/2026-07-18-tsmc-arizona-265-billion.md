summary: >-
  TSMC CEO C.C. Wei announced an additional $100 billion U.S. investment at
  the company's Q2 earnings call, lifting its total committed Arizona
  spending to $265 billion and adding at least four more fabs plus advanced
  packaging facilities for 2-nanometer-and-below chips. No construction
  timeline was set — Wei said pace will track market demand. The
  announcement came alongside TSMC's fifth consecutive record quarter: net
  income up 77.4% year over year and gross margin at a record 67.7%, with
  high-performance computing now 66% of revenue.
implications: >-
  The added packaging capacity matters more than the extra fab count —
  CoWoS packaging, not raw wafer output, has been the real bottleneck on AI
  accelerator supply, and on-site Arizona packaging would give TSMC's US
  customers a fully domestic pipeline from wafer to finished chip for the
  first time. Tying this expansion to record earnings rather than a
  subsidy signals TSMC sees the AI buildout as durable demand, not a
  bubble to hedge against.
