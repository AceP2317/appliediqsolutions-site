summary: >-
  White House OSTP Director Michael Kratsios said Wednesday that Moonshot AI
  "acquired GB300-equipped servers and has accessed GB300s in Thailand" to
  train its models, and that the US has information Moonshot "distilled
  Anthropic's Fable for the development of its K3 model" — despite Nvidia's
  most advanced chips being under export restriction to China. Treasury
  Secretary Scott Bessent said the same day that sanctions and Entity List
  designations would be "on the table" for firms whose distillation crosses
  into IP theft. Moonshot's Kimi K3, released Friday, is the largest
  open-weight model yet at 2.8 trillion parameters.
implications: >-
  It is the sharpest public escalation yet in the chip-export fight — a named
  US official accusing one company of both hardware smuggling and
  model-scale IP theft, with a same-day cabinet-level sanctions threat
  attached. For anyone evaluating open-weight Chinese models, a reminder
  that benchmark performance and supply-chain legality are now separate
  questions worth tracking separately.
