summary: >-
  OpenAI and Effingham County, Georgia officials jointly announced "Project
  Camellia," a data center campus OpenAI is designing and building itself
  for the first time, a $20 billion capital investment with total project
  costs potentially exceeding $30 billion. Local officials project 400
  long-term jobs and said proceeds could fund a full rollback of county
  homestead property taxes as soon as next year — OpenAI's shift from
  renting cloud capacity toward owning physical infrastructure outright.
implications: >-
  As labs move from leasing compute to building and owning it outright,
  frontier AI's economics increasingly resemble utility-scale infrastructure
  investment — multi-decade capital commitments underwritten by local tax
  deals — rather than pure software spending, worth watching for anyone
  estimating how durable current AI pricing and access will be.
