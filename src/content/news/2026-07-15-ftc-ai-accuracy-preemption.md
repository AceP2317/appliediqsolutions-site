title: 'FTC: state laws forcing AI models to alter their answers may be preempted'
publishedAt: '2026-07-15T06:05:00-04:00'
tier: 'standard'
spotlight: false
summary: >-
  The FTC issued a proposed policy statement, directed by a December
  executive order from President Trump, arguing that AI companies make
  implicit and explicit promises that their systems produce the most
  accurate output possible — so quietly distorting outputs to hit an
  undisclosed ideological target could be deceptive conduct under Section 5
  of the FTC Act. It singles out Colorado's Artificial Intelligence Act as a
  state law that "appears to coerce" companies into altering model outputs,
  and concludes such a law is "impliedly preempted to the extent it
  conflicts with a federal regulatory scheme." The Commission voted 2-0 to
  open public comment, open through July 31, 2026.
implications: >-
  This is a proposed policy statement, not a final rule, and the comment
  period is still open — but it sets up a direct federal-versus-state
  collision over who gets to dictate what an AI model is allowed to say.
  Any business relying on a vendor's model to answer questions, summarize
  documents, or make recommendations is exposed to whichever side of that
  fight wins in the states where it operates, through no choice of its own.
angle: >-
  A rented model's output behavior can be reshaped by a federal-state legal
  fight the business using it had no part in and can't opt out of. That
  particular exposure doesn't exist for software a company owns and
  controls outright.
